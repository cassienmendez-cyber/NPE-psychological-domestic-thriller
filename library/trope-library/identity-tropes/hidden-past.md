# HIDDEN PAST

## Core Concept
Character (protagonist or antagonist) is hiding their previous identity, crimes, trauma, or life circumstances. Past threatens to emerge and destroy current life. Creates tension between who they are now vs. who they were, and between truth and constructed identity.

## Psychological Foundation
Explores identity fluidity, redemption possibility, and consequences of running from past. Questions whether we can truly escape or reinvent ourselves. Examines cost of living a lie.

## Key Characteristics
- Character living under new identity or hiding past
- Built new life based on lies/omissions
- Past connections or evidence threatens exposure
- May involve: witness protection, escaped abuse, hidden crime, assumed identity, secret trauma
- Someone from past appears
- Character must choose between truth and current life
- Exposure threatens everything they've built

## Narrative Use
**Setup:** Establish current life and hints of hidden past
**Escalation:** Past intrudes, character tries to manage both identities, threat of exposure grows
**Payoff:** Truth revealed, consequences faced, integration or destruction

## Compatible Elements
✅ Works well with: Blackmail plots, witness protection, escaped abuse, social climbing, small-town settings
⚠️ Careful with: Making past crimes too heinous if protagonist, redemption vs. consequences balance
❌ Avoid: Easy acceptance of serious crimes, no consequences for deception

## Genre Fit
- Psychological thriller: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Domestic thriller: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Example Application
Perfect suburban mother is actually witness protection witness who testified against violent crime boss 15 years ago. When someone from her past appears in town, she must protect her family from both the criminal threat and the truth of who she really is. Her carefully constructed life and identity unravel as she's forced to become her former self to survive.
