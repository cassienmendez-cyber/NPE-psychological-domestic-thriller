# DISSOCIATION

## Core Concept
Protagonist experiences dissociative episodes where they lose time, don't remember actions, or feel detached from reality. Creates unreliable narration and questions about what really happened. May be symptom of trauma, mental illness, or drug-induced.

## Psychological Foundation
Based on real dissociative disorders (DID, depersonalization, derealization). Trauma response where mind protects itself by disconnecting. Creates narrative uncertainty about protagonist's actions and perceptions.

## Key Characteristics
- Protagonist loses chunks of time
- Doesn't remember important events or actions
- May have alternate personality states (DID)
- Feels detached from body or reality
- Others tell them things they did but don't remember
- May discover evidence of own actions they can't recall
- Questions whether they committed crime or harmed someone
- May be experiencing genuine disorder or being drugged

## Narrative Use
**Setup:** Establish protagonist's dissociative episodes early
**Escalation:** Lost time increases, consequences mount, protagonist fears what they've done
**Payoff:** Truth revealed (they did/didn't do it, cause of dissociation)

## Compatible Elements
✅ Unreliable narrator, trauma survivor protagonist, protagonist-did-it twist, gaslighting (someone exploiting condition)
⚠️ Respectful portrayal of dissociative disorders
❌ Exploitative use of mental illness, DID as villain trope

## Genre Fit
- Psychological thriller: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Domestic thriller: ⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Example Application
Trauma survivor protagonist has dissociative episodes with lost time. Husband is found dead. She has his blood on her but doesn't remember. Did she kill him in dissociative state? Or is someone drugging her and framing her? Investigation reveals truth about her condition and the night's events.
