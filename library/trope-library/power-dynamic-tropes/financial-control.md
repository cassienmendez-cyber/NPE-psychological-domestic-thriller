# FINANCIAL CONTROL

## Core Concept
One person controls all finances in relationship, creating dependency and trapping partner. Financial abuse as form of domestic control. Victim has no access to money, accounts, or economic independence, making escape nearly impossible.

## Psychological Foundation
Economic abuse creates practical barriers to leaving while reinforcing powerlessness. Money equals freedom and options. Control over finances extends control over victim's entire life.

## Key Characteristics
- Controller manages all accounts, gives allowance, monitors spending
- Victim has no credit cards, bank access, or independent income
- Pre-nup or financial agreements trap victim
- Sabotage of victim's employment or education
- Threat of financial ruin if victim leaves
- Children's needs used as leverage
- Victim's family may be financially dependent too

## Narrative Use
**Setup:** Establish financial arrangement, initially seems practical
**Escalation:** Victim realizes trap, attempts independence blocked
**Payoff:** Victim finds way to economic freedom or must leave with nothing

## Compatible Elements
✅ Trophy wife, controlling spouse, inheritance plots, divorce/custody battles
⚠️ Class dynamics, victim-blaming
❌ Easy money solution, glamorizing dependence

## Genre Fit
- Psychological thriller: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Domestic thriller: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Example Application
Wealthy husband controls all money, wife has no access. When she discovers his crimes and wants to leave, she has no resources. Pre-nup leaves her nothing. She must secretly build financial independence while planning escape—hiding money, securing job, gathering evidence. He monitors her spending, sabotages her attempts. Climax involves her escape despite financial control.
