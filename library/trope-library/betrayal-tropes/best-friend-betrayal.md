# BEST FRIEND BETRAYAL

## Core Concept
Protagonist's closest friend is secretly working against them—stealing their life, sabotaging them, or involved in conspiracy targeting them. The intimacy and trust of friendship makes betrayal particularly devastating. Protagonist must recognize betrayal while grieving loss of trusted relationship.

## Psychological Foundation
Exploits trust vulnerability in close friendships. Creates paranoia about who to trust. Questions how well we know anyone. Explores envy, resentment, and pathological relationship dynamics masked as friendship.

## Key Characteristics
- Long-term close friendship established
- Friend has access to intimate details, home, family, secrets
- Friend secretly undermines or sabotages protagonist
- May involve: envy, romantic rivalry, conspiracy, identity theft, hidden agenda
- Protagonist trusts friend implicitly, confides in them
- Clues of betrayal dismissed or explained away
- Revelation devastates protagonist emotionally
- Must fight friend who knows all their vulnerabilities

## Narrative Use
**Setup:** Establish close, trusted friendship; friend helps protagonist with emerging problem
**Escalation:** Friend's "help" actually makes things worse; protagonist confides, friend uses info against them
**Payoff:** Betrayal revealed, friendship destroyed, protagonist must overcome someone who knows them intimately

## Compatible Elements
✅ Works well with: Parasitic friend archetype, envious rival archetype, romantic triangles, conspiracy plots
⚠️ Careful with: Making all close friendships suspect, friendship stereotypes
❌ Avoid: Easy forgiveness, betrayal without proper setup, toxic friendship as healthy

## Genre Fit
- Psychological thriller: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- Domestic thriller: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

## Example Application
Protagonist's best friend since college seems supportive during protagonist's divorce. But friend is actually having affair with protagonist's husband, sabotaging her custody case, and stealing clients from her business—all while playing supportive friend. Reveal comes when protagonist discovers text messages or sees them together. Must fight both ex-husband and former best friend who knows all her weaknesses.
